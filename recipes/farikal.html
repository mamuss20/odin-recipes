Fårikål, Lamb Stew

The national dish of Norway: a mutton and cabbage stew perfect for cold days.

Recipe and photo credit: Salmon & Frogs

The official national dish in Norway, fårikål, consists of lamb meat on the bone that simmers for hours together with cabbage and pepper grains. This dish is so important in Norway that it is even honored with its own national day. the last Thursday in September. On this day Norwegians all over the world are likely to have fårikål for dinner. Traditionally this is served with boiled potatoes and you would drink beer and aquavit (a spirit distilled from grains and potatoes). Making a delicious, traditional meal can't be easier than this.

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs mutton or lamb (Sliced shoulder, neck, or breast on the bone)
  • 3 lbs cabbage
  • 4 tsp whole black peppercorns
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 17 oz water

Steps

  1. Cut the cabbage into large wedges.
  2. Layer meat and cabbage in a pot, starting with meat at the bottom. Sprinkle salt and pepper between the layers.
  3. Pour over the water. Bring to a boil, then lower temperature and let the pot simmer on low heat until the meat is so tender it let's go off the bone, about 2 hours.
  4. Serve with boiled potatoes.
